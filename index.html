Edward Snowden

Early Life

Rather than returning to school, he passed the GED test and took classes at Anne Arundel Community College. Although Snowden had no undergraduate college degree,he worked online toward a master's degree at the University of Liverpool, England, in 2011. He was interested in Japanese popular culture, had studied the Japanese language,and worked for an anime company that had a resident office in the U.S. He also said he had a basic understanding of Mandarin Chinese and was deeply interested in martial arts. At age 20, he listed Buddhism as his religion on a military recruitment form, noting that the choice of agnostic was "strangely absent".

Career and Contributions

Edward Snowden's career primarily involved working in the field of intelligence and technology. Here is a summary of his career:
1. **CIA Employment (2006-2009):**
Edward Snowden began his career in the intelligence field by working for the Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) as a technical expert. During this time, he was stationed in various locations, including Switzerland.
2. **NSA Contractor (2009-2013):**
Snowden left the CIA and started working for private companies contracted by the National Security Agency (NSA). Most notably, he was employed by Booz Allen Hamilton as an NSA contractor in Hawaii. It was during this time that he gained access to and subsequently leaked classified information about NSA surveillance programs.
3. **Whistleblowing (2013):**
Snowden became known worldwide for his whistleblowing activities in 2013 when he leaked a significant amount of classified documents to journalists, revealing the extent of global surveillance programs conducted by the NSA and its partners. These revelations had a profound impact on the fields of cybersecurity, privacy, and government surveillance.
4. **Exile and Advocacy (2013-present):**
After disclosing classified information, Snowden fled the United States and sought asylum in Russia, where he has been living since 2013. During his exile, he has continued to be an advocate for privacy rights and civil liberties, speaking at conferences and engaging in public discussions about surveillance and government transparency.

Edward Snowden's career is defined by his work in the intelligence community and his decision to blow the whistle on what he perceived as unconstitutional and invasive government surveillance practices, which had far-reaching consequences and sparked international debates on issues of privacy and security.


Edward Snowden's contributions are primarily related to his whistleblowing activities and their impact on the fields of privacy, civil liberties, and government surveillance:
1. **Exposing Mass Surveillance:**
Snowden's most significant contribution was exposing the extent of mass surveillance programs conducted by the National Security Agency (NSA) and its international partners. His leaks revealed the existence of programs like PRISM, which collected vast amounts of data from internet companies, and the bulk collection of phone metadata.
2. **Public Awareness:**
Snowden's disclosures brought these surveillance practices to the public's attention, leading to increased awareness about government intrusion into individuals' privacy. This awareness spurred public debate and discussions about the balance between national security and civil liberties.
3. **Legal Reforms:**
Snowden's revelations prompted legal and policy changes in the United States and other countries. The USA Freedom Act, for example, curtailed certain aspects of government data collection, and courts have ruled some surveillance practices as unconstitutional.
4. **Global Impact:**
Snowden's actions had a global impact, as they led to discussions about surveillance practices in many countries beyond the United States. Governments and tech companies worldwide reevaluated their own data collection and sharing practices in response to the revelations.
5. **Personal Sacrifice:**
Snowden's willingness to face legal consequences, including exile from his home country, is seen as a contribution to the principle of government accountability and transparency. He demonstrated the personal sacrifices that some whistleblowers are willing to make to bring issues of public concern to light.

Edward Snowden's contributions have shaped the way governments, companies, and individuals approach issues of privacy and surveillance in the digital age. His actions have had a lasting impact on the discourse surrounding civil liberties and government transparency.

Awards

Edward Snowden has received several awards and recognitions for his whistleblowing activities and his contributions to the debate on government surveillance and privacy. Some notable awards and honors include:
1. **Sam Adams Award (2013):**
Snowden was awarded the Sam Adams Award for Integrity in Intelligence, which is given annually by a group of retired CIA officers to individuals who demonstrate a commitment to truth-telling and integrity in the field of intelligence.
2. **Right Livelihood Award (2014):**
Snowden received the Right Livelihood Award, often referred to as the "Alternative Nobel Prize," for his courage and skill in revealing the unprecedented extent of state surveillance, which violates basic democratic processes and individual rights.
3. **Carl von Ossietzky Medal (2014):**
The Carl von Ossietzky Medal was awarded to Snowden by the International League for Human Rights for his extraordinary contributions to the defense of civil liberties and human rights.
4. **Whistleblower Prize (2019):**
Snowden was awarded the Whistleblower Prize by the German branch of the Association of German Scientists (VDW) and the German branch of the International Association of Lawyers Against Nuclear Arms (IALANA) for his exceptional contributions to the public interest by disclosing extensive, systemic surveillance practices.
5. **Glasgow University Rector (2014):**
While not an award, Snowden was elected as the rector of the University of Glasgow in absentia. This was a symbolic recognition of his contribution to the public discourse on surveillance and civil liberties.

It's important to note that Edward Snowden remains a controversial figure, and opinions about him and his actions vary widely. While some organizations and individuals have recognized him for his whistleblowing efforts, others consider him a traitor or a criminal for leaking classified information.
